## Biographical information (wiki)

- **James "Jim" Lahey.** Born April 12, 1946. Died October 17, 2017 (real life: actor John Dunsworth; in *Trailer Park Boys: The Animated Series*, eaten by a shithawk).
- **Portrayed by** John Dunsworth (Seasons 1–12, all three movies). Final live-action line: "Fuckin Fucked out of our fuckin minds." Season 12 finale includes a memorial and behind-the-scenes clip of John talking about kindness.
- **Aliases:** Jim, The Liquor, Sir, Mr. Lahey, Lim Jahey, Fucky the Drunk Clown, Liquorace (by Cyrus).
- **Career:** Police officer (1972–1977). Trailer park supervisor (1985–2004/2005–present in show). Describes himself as a "Crazy Drunk Fackin' Deranged Lunatic Who Used To Be A Cop."
- **Relationships:** Barb Lahey (ex-wife; her father owned Sunnyvale). Treena Lahey (daughter). Randy (partner / surrogate son; assistant supervisor, often in his underwear, cheeseburgers).

## Character background (origins)

- Second of ten children. Father was an alcoholic; once told young Jim in a drunken rant that "Men that drink shouldn't fucking drink!!!" and smashed his liquor bottle. Jim still called him "a nice man." Mother took him to Chester, Nova Scotia, at seven; she won bingo at a county fair and got him flippers.
- As a teenager he had gay thoughts but didn't tell his father for fear of being "spanked" and sent to Vietnam.
- **1970:** One-night stand with Tammy (Ray LaFleur's wife). Ricky was born around 1971 and raised by Ray; for years Lahey thought Ricky was his son and made his life hell.
- **Early 1970s:** Jim and George Green were police officers in Nova Scotia. Jim married Barb (her father owned Sunnyvale).
- **October 1977 (Halloween):** Ricky pushed Lahey's cop car as a prank; it crashed into a trailer. Julian splashed liquor on Lahey; Lahey took Julian's bottle and spilled it on his crotch walking back to the car. George Green arrived, saw the damage and liquor on Lahey's crotch, and thought he was drunk. Lahey threatened him; George arrested him. The story got out; Lahey sued, lost a year-long legal battle, and resigned from the force. George became police chief. That incident is a big reason Lahey despises the boys, especially Ricky.
- After that, his life went downhill; he drank heavily, and he and Barb divorced. He became supervisor of Sunnyvale (Barb had the park; he had law-enforcement connections). The boys (Ricky, Julian, Bubbles) pulled pranks on him—e.g. an arrow in the crotch at 16, Lahey out for seven hours. He called the cops on them constantly; they kept covering their tracks.

## Love life and Randy

- Barb also left because of his bisexuality: he'd been hiring "Smokey," a male prostitute—who was actually **Randy**, a former park resident. Barb (Christian, helping the less fortunate) invited "Randy" home for Christmas to "rescue" him ("Dear Santa Claus, Go Fuck Yourself").
- Randy stayed and became Lahey's assistant supervisor and partner. When caught in sexual role-play they claimed they were "rehearsing a play for the Blandford Recreation Centre." Eventually Randy outed them in front of the park; nobody cared except Ricky, who was confused.
- Lahey later showed attraction to Julian ("sexy"), and once made Julian kiss him to get his 1% of trailer park ownership. At one point Lahey, Barb, and Randy lived together until Barb got fed up with Randy (weed, cheating with Lucy, pregnancy); Randy left, Lahey went back to the bottle. Randy came back later ("Jump The Cheeseburger"). Lahey stayed sober for a while, then relapsed and Randy left again (*Trailer Park Boys: The Movie*). They reunite in "Don't Legalize It" (snooping through Ricky and Julian's trash, trip to Montreal, cocaine, etc.).
- When drunk he uses Randy's pet name **Bobandy** more often.

## Jim and the liquor (wiki)

- Being drunk is central to his character—"his *raison d'être*"—along with being park supervisor and ex-cop; the three are always in tension.
- He's a career alcoholic. He's said things like "I am the liquor" and that the liquor helps him think clearly and stay on the ball; he causes the most trouble for the criminal element when he's drunk. **Julian and Ricky's view:** *Jim + liquor = good for them*, *Jim - liquor = bad for them*—so they plan around his drinking.
- He's been homicidal and suicidal (institution in "Give Peace A Chance"); when released he claimed he quit but was drinking vodka from water bottles. In season five he labeled bottles of rum with park residents' names and drank a bottle when that person went to jail ("shit-puppets"). He's fallen down, passed out, blacked out (e.g. woke up with the car roof missing).
- **Episode titles / phrases:** "Let The Liquor Do The Thinking," "Jim Lahey Is A Fuckin' Drunk And He Always Will Be," "The Winds Of Shit," "Don't Cross The Shitline." He's worn khakis as supervisor; when things get worse he's in underwear and a robe.

## Shit talk and characterization (wiki)

- He uses **shit metaphors** constantly—shitstorm, shit winds, shit hawks, shitmobile, shit leopard, shit line, shit ship, etc. He warned Bubbles about "shit winds" before a break-in; Ricky blamed a urinal flush on the "shit winds" and they botched the job. The local police use similar shit metaphors.
- Like Ricky, he **misquotes** sayings and tries to sound intellectual; park residents don't correct him.
- **Quotes (from wiki):** "Randy, I am the liquor." "A liquor captain never abandons his sinking shit ship, Randy."
- He started the series as a swaggering, confident park supervisor; by season two he was deep into alcoholism and the mustache was gone. Main antagonist of the show; one of the few characters with a first and last name.

## Criminal activities (wiki)

- As cop and ex-cop he's not above breaking the law to get the boys: planting evidence, B&E, entrapment. Evidence planting (e.g. cocaine on Julian), secret camera in Julian's bathroom, frame-ups (e.g. Ted and George with aluminum bats). In "Propane, Propane" he tries to kill Ricky (cut brake line, net, electric turkey fryer, hot tub explosion). Downtown standoff: he tries to shoot/blow up Ricky. Later he bails out Cyrus, Dennis, and Terry hoping they'd kill Ricky.
- He's been arrested for DUI, public drunkenness ("Conky"), strapping dynamite to himself ("Workin' Man"), and once got sprayed with blue dye at an ATM and was jailed despite not being part of the heist ("A Shit Leopard Can't Change Its Spots").
